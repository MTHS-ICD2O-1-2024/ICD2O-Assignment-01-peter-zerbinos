History

John Edward Hopcroft, born in Seattle, Washington, is a renowned theoretical computer scientist.
He earned his bachelor's degree in electrical engineering from Seattle University in 1961 and later obtained his master's and Ph.D. from Stanford University.
Hopcroft worked at Princeton University before joining Cornell University, where he became a professor emeritus.
His textbooks on algorithms and formal languages, co-authored with Jeffrey Ullman and Alfred Aho, are considered classics in computer science.
In 1986, he was awarded the prestigious Turing Award for his contributions to algorithms and data structures.
He has also been recognized with honors such as the IEEE John von Neumann Medal and the Karl V. Karlstrom Outstanding Educator Award.
